D-NAV Definitions

The language and signals behind faster, clearer decisions.

Core ingredients (1–10 each)

Impact

The value at stake if this goes right. Higher = bigger upside/importance.

Cost

Resources you must spend: time, cash, attention, opportunity cost.

Risk

Downside uncertainty: likelihood × magnitude of harm or failure.

Urgency

Time pressure to act. High urgency compresses decision windows.

Confidence

Evidence and readiness to execute (proof, reps, clarity, resources).

Derived signals

Return

Impact − Cost
Value creation after cost: Positive Neutral Negative.

Stability

Confidence − Risk
How survivable the move is: Stable Fragile.

Pressure

Urgency − Confidence
Execution stress: Pressured Balanced Calm.
Order doesn’t change the math; we group them to highlight different risks: Return (unit economics), Stability (survivability), Pressure (execution risk). Short-term negative return can be strategic — keep stability positive and pressure managed.

Merit & Drive

Merit

Formula
Impact − Cost − Risk

The underlying quality of the bet (unit economics minus risk drag). “Is this inherently good?”

Drive

Formula
Urgency × Confidence

Your applied energy — how hard and how ready you’ll push right now.

Composite (D-NAV)

Formula
D-NAV = (Impact − Cost − Risk) + (Urgency × Confidence)

D-NAV combines Merit (quality of the opportunity) with Drive (your applied energy). High D-NAV = strong bet and/or strong push — but always read Return/Stability/Pressure to avoid hidden traps and slow bleeds.
