D-NAV Definitions

The language and signals behind faster, clearer decisions.

Core ingredients (1–10 each)

Impact

The value at stake if this goes right. Higher = bigger upside/importance.

Cost

Resources you must spend: time, cash, attention, opportunity cost.

Risk

Downside uncertainty: likelihood × magnitude of harm or failure.

Urgency

Time pressure to act. High urgency compresses decision windows.

Confidence

Evidence and readiness to execute (proof, reps, clarity, resources).

Derived signals

Return

Formula
Impact − Cost

Value creation after cost. Positive / Neutral / Negative.

Stability

Formula
Confidence − Risk

How survivable the move is. Stable vs Fragile.

Pressure

Formula
Urgency − Confidence

Execution stress. Pressured, Balanced, or Calm.

Merit & Attention

Merit

Formula
Impact − Cost − Risk

The underlying quality of the bet (unit economics + risk drag). Think “is this inherently good?”

Attention

Formula
Urgency × Confidence

Your applied drive—how hard and how ready you’ll push right now.

Composite (D-NAV)

Formula
D-NAV = (Impact − Cost − Risk) + (Urgency × Confidence)

D-NAV combines Merit (quality of the opportunity) with Attention (your applied energy). High D-NAV = strong bet and/or strong push.
