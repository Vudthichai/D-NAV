Core ingredients (1–10 each)

Impact

Upside/importance if it works.

Cost

Time • money • effort • focus.

Risk

Downside = likelihood × size of harm.

Urgency

How soon action is needed.

Confidence

Evidence & readiness to execute.

Derived signals

Return

Impact − Cost
Value after cost: Positive Neutral Negative.

Stability

Confidence − Risk
Survivability: Stable Uncertain Fragile.

Pressure

Urgency − Confidence
Execution stress: Pressured Balanced Calm.
Order doesn’t change the math; we group them to highlight different risks: Return (unit economics), Stability (survivability), Pressure (execution risk). Short-term negative return can be strategic — keep stability ≥ 0 and pressure managed.

Merit & Drive

Merit

Formula
Impact − Cost − Risk

Inherent quality of the bet (unit economics minus risk drag).

Drive

Formula
Urgency × Confidence

Applied energy — how hard & how ready you’ll push now.

Composite (D-NAV)

Formula
D-NAV = (Impact − Cost − Risk) + (Urgency × Confidence)

D-NAV blends Merit (quality of the bet) with Drive (execution momentum). High D-NAV = a strong bet and/or a strong push — but always read Return, Stability, and Pressure to avoid hidden traps and slow bleeds.

Archetypes (Pressure • Stability • Return)

Each outcome is defined by the signs of Pressure, Stability, and Return → e.g., P+, S+, R+.
