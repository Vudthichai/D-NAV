D-NAV Definitions

The language and signals behind faster, clearer decisions.

Core ingredients (1–10 each)

Impact

Upside/importance if it works.

Cost

Time • money • effort • focus.

Risk

Downside (likelihood × magnitude of harm).

Urgency

How soon action is needed.

Confidence

Evidence & readiness to execute.

Notes: 1 = minimal • 10 = extreme. Scores are calibrated per user/team over time.

Derived signals

Return

Return = Impact − Cost
Value after cost: Positive Neutral Negative.

Stability

Stability = Confidence − Risk
Survivability: Stable Uncertain Fragile.

Pressure

Pressure = Urgency − Confidence
Execution stress: Pressured Balanced Calm.
We group them to expose different risks: Return (unit economics), Stability (survivability), Pressure (execution risk). Short-term negative return can be strategic — keep stability ≥ 0 and pressure managed.

Merit & Drive

Merit

Formula
Merit = Impact − Cost − Risk

Inherent quality of the bet (unit economics minus risk drag).

Drive

Formula
Drive = Urgency × Confidence

Applied energy — how hard & how ready you’ll push now.

Composite (D-NAV)

Formula
D-NAV = (Impact − Cost − Risk) + (Urgency × Confidence)

D-NAV blends Merit (quality of the bet) with Drive (execution momentum). High D-NAV = a strong bet and/or a strong push — always read Return, Stability, and Pressure to avoid hidden traps and slow bleeds.

Compare Mode

Side-by-side comparison between a Base scenario and a Scenario you adjust with sliders.

Delta (Δ)
ΔX = XScenario − XBase. Positive = Scenario is higher.
ΔReturn
Δ(Impact − Cost) — net value change after cost.
ΔStability
Δ(Confidence − Risk) — survivability change.
ΔPressure
Δ(Urgency − Confidence) — execution stress change.
ΔD-NAV
Δ[(Impact − Cost − Risk) + (Urgency × Confidence)] — overall quality × push change.

Smallest Nudge (optimizer hint)

The “Smallest Nudge” recommends the single 1-point slider change that yields the largest ΔD-NAV while avoiding red flags (e.g., pushing Pressure deeply positive or Stability negative). It’s a local, minimal-effort move, not a global optimum.

Narrative insight (2 lines) explains why ΔD-NAV moved: e.g., “Return up via +Impact (cost unchanged), Stability flat; pressure increases slightly — acceptable if short-term.”

Learning Curve & Momentum

Turn outcomes into improvement. The system tracks short-, mid-, and long-horizon learning signals from your decision stream.

Learning Curve Index (LCI)
Measures recovery after drawdowns: how effectively you rebound from a local low. LCI = Rebound / Drawdown. >1.0 = over-recovery; ≈1.0 = full recovery; <1.0 = under-recovery.
Momentumn
Trend velocity over the last n decisions using least-squares slope of a rolling mean. Momentumn = slope(MAn(D-NAV)). Also available per signal: Momentumn(Return|Stability|Pressure).
MA / EMA
MAn(X) = rolling average of the last n. EMAn(X) = exponential average (faster to react). UI uses MA by default; EMA is optional.
Post-loss bounce
For sequences with negative Return or D-NAV dips, LCI highlights rebound efficiency until you surpass the pre-dip baseline.
Defaults: short = 15 decisions, mid = 50, long = 100. You can change windows in Settings. Short = “steering now,” mid = “course,” long = “climate.”

Rolling Windows

Short (15)

Captures immediate behavior shifts and learning. Good for rapid feedback loops and testing nudges.

Mid (50)

Stabilizes noise; shows whether tactics are compounding or stalling.

Long (100)

Baseline trend and resilience over time. Useful for category benchmarking and seasonality.

Why 15? It’s long enough to smooth randomness but short enough for actionable course-correction in a typical weekly cadence. If your cadence is slower/faster, adjust in Settings.

Category Stats

Every decision belongs to a category (e.g., Hiring, Fitness, Capital Allocation). D-NAV summarizes per-category behavior to reduce self-mythology.

Category Avg
Avgcat(D-NAV), plus Avgcat(Return|Stability|Pressure).
Category Momentum
Momentumncat(D-NAV) — trend velocity within the category.
Diff vs Personal
ΔD-NAVcat = Avgcat(D-NAV) − Avgall(D-NAV) — where you over/underperform yourself.
Baselines
Optional fixed baselines (e.g., “ship rate ≥ 3/wk”) used in narratives and flags.
Reports show: category ranks, trends, and risk posture distributions (P/S/R sign mix) so you can rebalance attention and tactics.

Thresholds & Flags

Return
> 0 Gain; = 0 Flat; < 0 Loss.
Stability
≥ 0 Stable; ≈ 0 Uncertain; < 0 Fragile.
Pressure
< 0 Calm; ≈ 0 Balanced; > 0 Pressured.
Narratives
Tooltips and compare summaries surface causes: e.g., “Return ↑ via Impact; Pressure ↑ due to Urgency outpacing Confidence.”

Archetypes (Pressure • Stability • Return)

Each outcome is defined by the signs of Pressure, Stability, and Return → e.g., P+, S+, R+.

Notation

ΔX
Delta (change). In Compare: XScenario − XBase. In time series: Xt − Xt−1.
MAn(X)
Moving average over the last n decisions.
EMAn(X)
Exponential moving average (more weight on recent points).

Least-squares slope of MAn(X) (positive = trending up).
